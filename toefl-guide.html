TOEFL iBT Complete Guide

Master every section: Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing. Strategies, scoring, time management, and pro tips — all in one ultimate resource.

What is TOEFL iBT?

Test of English as a Foreign Language (Internet-Based Test) measures academic English proficiency. Used by 11,500+ universities in 160+ countries. Score range: 0–120 (30 per section). Valid for 2 years.

Accepted globally

Test Format & Duration

Total: ~3 hours (plus 10-min break). Reading: 54-72 min (3-4 passages), Listening: 41-57 min, Speaking: 17 min, Writing: 50 min. Integrated tasks simulate real academic environment.

Computer-delivered

1. Reading Section

Question Types

  • Factual / Negative Factual
  • Inference & Rhetorical Purpose
  • Vocabulary in Context
  • Sentence Insertion
  • Prose Summary (6 options, pick 3)
  • Fill in a Table (categories)

Key Strategies

✔ Skim passage for main idea (3-4 min) before answering.
✔ Highlight transition words (however, therefore).
✔ Eliminate 2 wrong answers quickly.
✔ For summary questions: focus on major ideas, not details.

🔹 10 question types mastery
Dr. Waleed’s Insider Reading Tip: “Never read the whole passage twice. Annotate keywords, then jump to questions. Use the ‘review’ button to flag uncertain answers.”

2. Listening Section

Lecture & Conversation Types

2-3 lectures (each 3–5 min, 6 questions) + 2-3 conversations (2–3 min, 5 questions). Accents: North American, British, Australian. Topics: arts, life science, physical science, social science.

Active Listening Strategies

✔ Take structured notes: main idea, attitude, transitions.
✔ Predict questions while listening (why professor says X?).
✔ Pay attention to repetition, blackboard cues, and student questions.
✔ Don’t rely on memory — note details.

Speaking pro tip: Use a template for integrated tasks: “The reading states that... However, the speaker argues that... because...” Always include transitional phrases.

4. Writing Section (2 tasks)

Integrated Writing (20 min)

Read a short academic passage → listen to a lecture → write 150–225 words summarizing lecture points and how they relate to reading. No personal opinion.

Structure: Introduction + 3 body paragraphs

Academic Discussion / Independent Writing (30 min)

New “Writing for an Academic Discussion” task (replaces independent essay). Respond to a professor's online discussion post, providing opinion and reasoning. 100+ words, clear argument, and evidence.

🔥 Updated TOEFL format 2023+

Scoring & Band Descriptors

1

Reading/Listening (raw → scaled)

Each correct answer gives 1 point raw. Scaled to 0–30. High scores require 85%+ accuracy.

2

Speaking (0–30)

Raters evaluate delivery, language use, topic development. Score based on 4 tasks averaged. Score 26+ = advanced.

3

Writing (0–30)

Integrated: how well you synthesize; Academic discussion: clarity, grammar, relevance. Automated e-rater + human rater.

Dr. Waleed's 6-Week Study Roadmap

Week 1-2: Foundation — learn question patterns, take 1 diagnostic test, identify weak sections. Focus on vocabulary 500 academic words.
Week 3-4: Skill building — daily reading 1 passage + listening lecture. Speaking templates & writing outlines.
Week 5: Full mock tests (3x) with strict timing. Analyze mistakes.
Week 6: Refine weak areas, practice integrated tasks, simulated environment, confidence drills.

20+ practice tests recommended
Avoid these TOEFL killers:
  • Not managing time — running out of reading section due to overthinking.
  • Speaking too quietly or using fillers “um” repeatedly.
  • Writing off-topic in integrated task: add personal opinion (forbidden).
  • Ignoring note-taking during listening lectures — key details missed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TOEFL easier than IELTS?

Both are similar in difficulty. TOEFL uses more academic content and multiple-choice reading/listening. IELTS offers more variety (maps, diagrams). Choose based on your strengths.

Can I take notes during the test?

Yes! ETS provides scratch paper. Use abbreviations & symbols for speed. Note-taking is essential for listening, speaking, integrated writing.

How many times can I retake TOEFL?

Unlimited, but you must wait at least 3 days between attempts. Most universities take the highest score.
