Dr. Waleed's Core Strategies

Reading Mastery

  • Skim passage first (3-4 min) for main idea
  • Identify question types: factual, inference, vocabulary, summary
  • Eliminate 2 wrong answers before choosing
  • For summary questions: focus on major ideas, not details
  • Use context clues for vocabulary questions

Listening Excellence

  • Take structured notes: main idea, details, speaker attitude
  • Watch for transitions: "however," "first," "the key point"
  • Predict questions while listening
  • Focus on lecture organization: problem-solution, cause-effect
  • Practice with 1.25x speed for stamina

Speaking Formula

  • Template: Opinion + Reason + Example + Conclusion
  • Use transition words: first, moreover, for instance
  • Record yourself and eliminate filler words (um, uh)
  • Aim for 120-150 words per 45-60 seconds
  • Practice with timer daily

Writing Framework

  • Integrated: Summarize lecture + reading relationship
  • Academic Discussion: Clear opinion + specific examples
  • Use academic vocabulary and varied sentence structures
  • Always proofread for grammar errors
  • Aim for 150-225 words (Integrated) / 100+ words (Discussion)

⏱️ Time Management Mastery

Reading: 18-20 min per passage
Listening: No pauses — stay focused
Speaking: 15-30 sec prep, 45-60 sec speak
Writing: Integrated 20 min, Discussion 30 min

Pro Tip: Always leave 2-3 minutes at the end of each section to review answers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many times can I take the TOEFL?
You can take the TOEFL iBT as many times as you wish, but you must wait at least 3 days between attempts. Most universities consider your highest score.
What score do I need for top universities?
Top universities typically require 100+ (out of 120). Ivy League schools often expect 105-110. Many graduate programs require 90-100.
How long should I prepare for TOEFL?
Most students need 2-3 months of consistent study (10-15 hours/week). Our 6-week intensive plan is designed for motivated learners.
Is the TOEFL harder than IELTS?
Both are similar in difficulty. TOEFL uses more academic content and multiple-choice format. IELTS offers more variety (maps, diagrams). Choose based on your strengths.
Can I take notes during the test?
Yes! ETS provides scratch paper and pencils. Note-taking is essential, especially for Listening, Speaking, and Integrated Writing tasks.
