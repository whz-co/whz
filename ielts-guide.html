Listening Module

4 sections · 40 questions · 30 minutes

Band 7+ Strategy

Section 1: Conversation

  • Social situation (2 speakers)
  • Topics: accommodation, travel, events
  • Focus: names, dates, numbers
  • 10 questions · easiest section

Section 2: Monologue

  • One speaker (guide/representative)
  • Topics: tours, facilities, services
  • Focus: locations, prices, features
  • Often includes map labeling

Section 3: Discussion

  • Academic conversation (2-4 speakers)
  • Topics: assignments, research, projects
  • Focus: opinions, agreements, plans
  • Most challenging section

Section 4: Lecture

  • Academic lecture (one speaker)
  • Topics: science, history, society
  • Focus: main ideas, details
  • 10 questions · continuous listening
Dr. Waleed's Golden Tip: Before each section, read ahead. Use the 30-second breaks to preview upcoming questions. Underline keywords - names, dates, places - so you know exactly what to listen for.

📝 Sample Question Types

Form Completion (Section 1)
Customer Name: _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
Phone Number: _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
Date of Booking: _ _ _ _ / _ _ / _ _
Map Labeling (Section 2)
"The reception is located at the main entrance. To your left is the ..."
Multiple Choice (Section 3)
Why do the students choose this topic?
A) It's easy to research
B) Professor recommended it
C) It relates to their previous work ✓

Reading Module

3 passages · 40 questions · 60 minutes

Skimming & Scanning

Passage 1

  • 2-3 short texts
  • Factual, descriptive
  • Topics: advertisements, timetables
  • 13-14 questions

Passage 2

  • Longer text (2-3 pages)
  • Work-related/training focus
  • More complex vocabulary
  • 13-14 questions

Passage 3

  • Long, complex text
  • Academic/abstract topic
  • Detailed arguments
  • 13-14 questions

Reading Strategies by Dr. Waleed

  • Skimming (2 min/passage): Read title, headings, first sentences of each paragraph
  • Scanning (1 min): Find keywords from questions in the text
  • Time Management: 20 minutes per passage maximum
  • Question Order: Questions usually follow text order
Vocabulary Building: Create a notebook of academic words. Group by theme: education, environment, technology, health. Review daily.

Writing Module

2 tasks · 60 minutes

Task 1: 20 min · Task 2: 40 min
Task 1 (Academic): Visual Data Description
Structure (150 words):

1. Introduction (1-2 sentences): Paraphrase the question
"The line graph illustrates the changes in ..."

2. Overview (2-3 sentences): Main trends (no numbers)
"Overall, there was a significant increase in ..."

3. Details Paragraph 1: Describe first main feature
4. Details Paragraph 2: Describe second feature/comparison
Task 1 (General Training): Letter Writing
Formal Letter Opening:
"Dear Sir/Madam,"
"I am writing to express my concern about ..."

Informal Letter Opening:
"Dear [Name],"
"Hope you're doing well! I'm writing to invite you to ..."

Always include: Purpose, details, required action
Task 2: Essay Writing (250 words)
4-Paragraph Structure:

Introduction (40 words):
- Hook + background
- Thesis statement (your opinion)

Body Paragraph 1 (90 words):
- Topic sentence
- Explanation + example

Body Paragraph 2 (90 words):
- Second point with example

Conclusion (30 words):
- Summarize + restate opinion
Dr. Waleed's Writing Formula: C-E-E (Claim - Explanation - Example). Every paragraph must have all three elements. Never write without an example!

✍️ Common Essay Topics

Education
"Some people believe that university education should be free for all. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
Technology
"The use of social media is replacing face-to-face interaction. Is this a positive or negative development?"
Environment
"Individual actions cannot solve environmental problems; only governments can. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Speaking Module

3 parts · 11-14 minutes

Fluency First

Part 1: Introduction (4-5 min)

  • Personal questions
  • Topics: work, study, home, hobbies
  • Answer: 2-3 sentences each
  • Show vocabulary naturally

Part 2: Long Turn (3-4 min)

  • Cue card topic
  • 1 minute preparation
  • Speak 1-2 minutes
  • Follow all prompts on card

Part 3: Discussion (4-5 min)

  • Abstract questions
  • Society, trends, future
  • Give opinions + reasons
  • Compare different views

🎯 Sample Cue Card (Part 2)

Describe a memorable journey you have taken.

You should say:
- Where you went
- Who you went with
- What happened during the journey
- And explain why it was memorable
Preparation Notes (1 minute):
• Where: Mountains of Kurdistan
• Who: Family, summer 2022
• What: Beautiful views, picnic, stories
• Why: First time seeing mountains, family bonding
Dr. Waleed's Fluency Secret: Don't worry about perfect grammar. Keep talking! Use fillers naturally: "That's an interesting question...", "Well, let me think...", "I suppose..."

💬 Part 3 Discussion Topics

Travel & Tourism
"How has tourism changed in your country over the last decade? What are the positive and negative impacts?"
Technology & Society
"Do you think technology makes people more isolated? Why or why not?"
